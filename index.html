Work

I love to be business minded and customer obsessed. My favorite part of my job is defining the product vision and getting creative during the design thinking process. I have a demonstrated ability to define the strategy, distilling requirements, and launching products. Through my prior roles, I also have a proven track record of working across many multidisciplinary teams and cross-functional projects with tight timelines and high visibility.

Since June 2018, I have been working at Microsoft on the Microsoft365 (M365) Machine Learning platform. The platform enables features like smart text prediction, search, meeting insights, and more. Specifically, I drove the entire offline training user experience design and journey of the self-service platform. In addition, I expanded training support from North America to Asia Pacific and Europe regions. Lastly, I designed and implemented core features related to data extraction of M365 datasets, maintaining GDPR compliance, and Azure resource provisioning.

Prior to my current role, I had some experience as a PM in the IoT networking space for Microsoft, software engineer intern at JP Morgan Chase, and as a software quality assurance engineer intern at Virgin Pulse. Download my resume to learn more.

Hack

I believe that investing in education will ultimately eliminate poverty and is the best investment for the future. This is why, almost 4 years ago, I started working with Social Experiments, a non-profit focused on digital transformation in the education space.

In partnership with the non-profit, I led projects in 5 different hackathons, winning $5,500 during 2 Hack For Good competitions. Our project got so much traction and visibility, the company actually sponsored a trip for me and a team member to go pilot our solutions at a few girls schools in Ghana. We partnered with GIZ's #eSkills4Girls initiative, and jp.ik, a leader in the digital education space, to pilot an offline Content Access Point(CAP) that hosts educational content while also having the capability to connect to Azure's cloud. By integrating Azure IoT Edge, we were able to update the previously dark CAP device with content and send back usage data when connected via ethernet or cellular.

Curious to learn more about the project? Watch the video I put together about our solution.

Do you work on a cool project in the education space? I'd love to help.
